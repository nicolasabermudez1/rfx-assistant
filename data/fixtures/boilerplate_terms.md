# Centrica plc — Standard Terms (extract for RFP IT-INF-DC-COOL-2026)

The following clauses are Centrica's mandatory boilerplate. Suppliers must accept verbatim or log a deviation in the Deviation Register.

## 1. Liability cap

The Supplier's aggregate liability under or in connection with this Contract (whether in contract, tort (including negligence), breach of statutory duty or otherwise) shall not exceed **125% of the Charges paid or payable in the twelve (12) months immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim**, save in respect of:

(a) death or personal injury caused by negligence;
(b) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
(c) breach of confidentiality obligations;
(d) breach of data protection obligations;
(e) any indemnity given under Clause 14 (Intellectual Property) or Clause 22 (Modern Slavery).

For the avoidance of doubt, loss of profits, loss of revenue, loss of anticipated savings and loss of business opportunity are recoverable in the Excluded Items above.

## 2. Service credits

Service credits shall apply at the following rates against the monthly Service Charges for any breach of the 4-hour Critical Response or 8-hour Critical Fix SLAs:

| Breach | Service credit |
|---|---|
| Each Critical Response SLA breach | 7.5% of the monthly Service Charges |
| Each Critical Fix SLA breach | 12.5% of the monthly Service Charges |
| Three or more Critical Fix breaches in a rolling quarter | Customer right to terminate without compensation |

Service credits are a price adjustment, not liquidated damages, and do not limit the Customer's other remedies.

## 3. Termination for convenience

The Customer may terminate this Contract or any Order at any time on **ninety (90) days' written notice** without cause. The Supplier shall be entitled to payment for Services properly performed up to the effective termination date, and to recover documented, unavoidable, third-party committed costs not exceeding **3% of the remaining unperformed contract value**. No further compensation is payable.

## 4. Step-in rights

Where (a) the Supplier commits a Material Default that is not remedied within 20 Business Days, or (b) the Supplier suffers an Insolvency Event, or (c) cooling availability falls below 99.50% over any rolling 30-day period, the Customer shall have the right to step in (directly or through a third party) and operate or maintain the relevant cooling assets at the Supplier's reasonable cost, without prejudice to other remedies.

## 5. Modern Slavery

The Supplier warrants that no part of the Services or the goods supplied is performed using slavery, servitude, forced or compulsory labour, or human trafficking (as defined in the Modern Slavery Act 2015). The Supplier shall provide Centrica with its annual Modern Slavery statement and a tier-2 supply chain disclosure for chiller compressors, refrigerant supply, and rare-earth components.

## 6. Sustainability covenants

The Supplier shall:
(a) maintain a public commitment to net zero Scope 1+2 emissions by 2040 or earlier;
(b) report annually against the agreed Lifecycle Carbon baseline;
(c) not exceed the make-up water consumption target of **1.6 L/kWh of IT load** averaged over any rolling 12-month period;
(d) phase out all refrigerants with GWP > 150 from the supplied estate by **31 December 2030**.

## 7. Data, cyber & OT security

The cooling controls platform constitutes an operational technology (OT) asset under Centrica's Critical Infrastructure Cyber Policy (CICP-2025-03). The Supplier shall maintain:
(a) IEC 62443 SL-2 alignment as a minimum;
(b) a documented patching cadence of ≤ 30 days for security patches once released by the controls vendor;
(c) network segregation from the Supplier's corporate IT and from the internet;
(d) annual third-party penetration testing with results disclosed to Centrica.

## 8. Insurance

The Supplier shall maintain, at its own cost, throughout the Term and for six years thereafter:
- Employer's Liability Insurance: not less than £10m per occurrence
- Public Liability Insurance: not less than £25m per occurrence and in aggregate
- Professional Indemnity Insurance: not less than £25m per occurrence
- Product Liability Insurance: not less than £10m per occurrence

## 9. Audit & step-in

Centrica or its appointed auditor (currently KPMG) may, on 10 Business Days' notice, audit the Supplier's compliance with this Contract no more than twice per Contract Year, save where a Material Default is reasonably suspected (in which case unlimited audits apply).

## 10. Change of control

The Supplier shall notify the Customer in writing within 5 Business Days of any change of Control of the Supplier or its Group. The Customer may, within 60 days of such notice, terminate this Contract without liability if the change of Control results in the Supplier coming under the Control of (a) a competitor of Centrica, (b) an entity sanctioned under UK or EU sanctions law, or (c) an entity headquartered in a jurisdiction listed by the FATF as high-risk.
